*IDEAS* 
* Caregiver
* "After the Diagnosis" — The Lost Month Nobody Designs For
When someone gets a serious diagnosis (cancer, MS, diabetes), the medical system kicks in — but the first 30 days are a complete UX disaster. People are drowning in paperwork, insurance calls, and decisions while in psychological shock. Nobody maps this experience or builds for it.
You build: An AI companion that sits with the patient in that window — not giving medical advice, but handling the logistics chaos: appointment sequencing, insurance jargon translation, question prep for doctor visits. The insight is that administrative burden is a health outcome.
* "Second Language, First Day" — Immigrant Kids in School Systems
Immigrant children often arrive mid-year with no language support and get placed based on age, not ability. Teachers have no context on them. The kid becomes invisible in the classroom for months.
You build: A lightweight "arrival profile" tool — filled out by the family in their native language using voice — that gives the teacher an instant, human picture of the child: what they were learning before, what they love, what traumatized them, what they're proud of. Not a bureaucratic record — a human introduction.

* The Twist Nobody Will Think Of: Build for the Caregiver, Not the Patient
Every team that touches this space will build for the sick person. But the most broken human in that 30-day window is actually the spouse, adult child, or sibling who just became an unpaid full-time medical coordinator overnight — while still going to work, hiding their panic, and pretending to be strong.
They have no onboarding. No training. No tool. And they're making life-altering decisions on 4 hours of sleep.

* "The Call I'm Dreading" — Rehearsal mode
Caregivers have to make terrible phone calls: fighting insurance denials, telling employers they need leave, calling distant relatives. The app lets them rehearse the conversation with AI — including pushback — before making the real call. This is something no healthcare app does.
🫂 "You're Not the Only One" — Hyperlocal peer matching
Not a support group. Not a forum. Just: "There are 3 people within 2 miles of you who did exactly this 18 months ago and came out the other side. Want an intro?" Warm, specific, human.
